Transparent Matter

How do you visualize something that can't be seen? The same way you discover it: by visualizing the effect that it has on the stuff that can be seen.
Normally, a graph like the one on the right would be used to show the effects of Dark Matter that lead to its discovery: the velocity of stars in the outskirts of the galaxy does not decrease as it would be expected if all its matter was the luminous matter, the matter which we see.
There is 5 times more DARK MATTER in the Universe, than regular matter, the kind that the stars, the planets, and us. The animation shows the motion of stars in a galaxy (similar to M33, where Dark Matter was discovered) with no dark matter, and in a dark matter potential (pseudo-isothermal potential)
